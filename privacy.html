Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026  ·  Effective: March 2026

Phalanx ("we", "us", or "our") is an open-source project. This policy explains what information we collect when you visit usephalanx.com and how we use it.

1. Information We Collect

We collect minimal information:

  • Email address — only if you voluntarily join the waitlist or contact us directly.
  • Usage analytics — aggregate, anonymous page-view data (no personal identifiers).
  • GitHub interactions — if you star, fork, or file issues on our public repository, GitHub's own privacy policy applies.

2. Self-Hosted Deployments

If you self-host Phalanx using Docker Compose, all data (Postgres, Redis, logs) remains entirely on your infrastructure. We have no access to your deployment data, API keys, code, or agent outputs.

3. How We Use Your Email

If you join the waitlist, we use your email only to:

  • Send onboarding information and early access details.
  • Notify you of major releases relevant to your signup.

We will never sell, rent, or share your email with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Cookies

We do not use tracking cookies. No third-party advertising cookies are used.

5. Third-Party Services

The website uses Google Fonts (loaded from Google's CDN). Google may collect minimal request metadata per their own privacy policy.

6. Data Retention

Waitlist email addresses are retained until you request deletion. You may request removal at any time by messaging @usephalanx on X.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to access, correct, or delete any personal information we hold about you. Contact us at @usephalanx or file an issue on GitHub.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy. Material changes will be announced on @usephalanx.

9. Contact

Questions? Reach us on X at @usephalanx or open an issue on GitHub.
